## Part 1: Finding Leads (The Scraper)

### What You're Looking For

Target local service businesses with outdated websites. These businesses have money but don't understand technology. Perfect targets:

**High-Value Categories:**
- HVAC companies (heating, cooling, plumbing)
- Electricians
- Roofers
- Landscapers
- Auto repair shops
- Dental offices
- Law firms (small practices)

**Why These Work:**
- They charge $100-300/hour for services
- They rely on local customers finding them online
- They have cash flow but terrible websites
- They don't know how to fix it themselves

### Step-by-Step: Scraping Leads

**Step 1:** Log into Velvet Alchemy and click **"Command Center"** in the navigation menu.

**Step 2:** Click **"Launch Scraper"** button (blue button at the top).

**Step 3:** Fill in the scraper form:
- **Business Type:** Enter one category (e.g., "electrician", "roofer", "HVAC")
- **Location:** Enter city and state (e.g., "Reno, Nevada" or "Austin, Texas")
- **Max Results:** Start with 50 leads

**Step 4:** Click **"Start Scraping"** and wait 2-5 minutes.

**Step 5:** Go to **"Leads"** page to see your new leads.

### Pro Tips for Scraping

**Start Local:** Begin with your own city so you can meet clients in person if needed. This builds trust faster.

**Focus on One Category:** Don't mix electricians and dentists. Specialize in one category per week so you can refine your pitch.

**Scrape in Batches:** Do 50 leads at a time, audit the best ones, then scrape more. Don't overwhelm yourself with 500 leads on day one.

**Avoid Chains:** Skip big franchises (they have corporate web teams). Target independent, family-owned businesses.

## Part 2: Pre-Screening Leads (The Quick Filter)

### Why Pre-Screen?

Not every lead is worth your time. Pre-screening scores leads 0-100 based on technical issues (SSL, page speed, mobile-friendly) **without** using expensive AI. This helps you focus on the best opportunities first.

### Step-by-Step: Pre-Screening

**Step 1:** Go to **"Command Center"**.

**Step 2:** Click **"Pre-Screen All (Quick)"** button.

**Step 3:** Wait 30-60 seconds (it's fast because no AI is involved).

**Step 4:** Go to **"Leads"** page and sort by **"Priority"** (dropdown at top).

### Understanding Priority Scores

| Score Range | What It Means | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| **75-100** | High-value target. Website has multiple technical issues. | Audit immediately |
| **50-74** | Medium-value target. Some issues but not urgent. | Audit if you have time |
| **0-49** | Low-value target. Website is decent or business is too small. | Skip for now |

**Color Coding:**
- **Green (75+):** Hot leads—audit these first
- **Yellow (50-74):** Warm leads—audit if you run out of green ones
- **Red (<50):** Cold leads—skip unless desperate

### Pro Tips for Pre-Screening

**Focus on Green:** Only audit leads scoring 75+ until you've closed your first 5 deals. Don't waste time on yellow/red leads.

**Check the URL:** Before auditing, click "View Site" to see if the business is still operating. If the website is completely broken or offline, skip it (they might be out of business).

**Look for Phone Numbers:** Businesses with visible phone numbers on their site are easier to contact. Prioritize these.

## Part 3: Running Full Audits (The Money Proof)

### What the Audit Does

The full audit uses GPT-4o Vision to analyze the website like a human would. It calculates:
- **Prestige Score (0-100):** How professional the website looks
- **Revenue Loss ($X,XXX/year):** How much money they're losing due to poor design
- **Technical Leaks:** Missing CTAs, slow load times, non-mobile-friendly, no SSL
- **Competitor Gaps:** How their site compares to top-ranked competitors

This audit report is your **proof** that they need to hire you.

### Step-by-Step: Running Audits

**Step 1:** Go to **"Leads"** page.

**Step 2:** Click the checkbox next to 3-5 high-priority leads (green scores).

**Step 3:** Click **"Audit Selected"** button (max 5 leads at a time).

**Step 4:** Wait 3-5 minutes per lead (you'll see progress: "2 of 5 complete").

**Step 5:** When complete, you'll see:
- **Prestige Score** displayed on each lead card
- **Status** changed to "audited" (green badge)

### Understanding Audit Results

**Prestige Score Guide:**

| Score | Revenue Loss | Pitch Angle |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| **0-40** | $20k-30k/year | "Your website is costing you $2k/month in lost customers" |
| **41-60** | $15k-25k/year | "You're losing $1,500/month because your site looks outdated" |
| **61-75** | $10k-18k/year | "Small fixes could recover $1,200/month in revenue" |
| **76-100** | $5k-10k/year | "Your site is decent, but competitors are beating you" |

**Best Targets:** Score 40-70 (they have obvious problems but the business is still functional).

### Step-by-Step: Viewing Detailed Reports

**Step 1:** Click on any audited lead to open the detail page.

**Step 2:** Click **"View Detailed Report"** button (purple button).

**Step 3:** The Report Drawer will slide open showing:
- **Digital Health Grade (A-F)**
- **Visual Assessment** (what looks good/bad)
- **Technical Performance** (load speed, mobile-friendly)
- **Revenue Leaks** (missing CTAs, unclear headlines, no social proof)
- **Competitive Gap Analysis** (how they compare to top competitors)
- **Recommended Action** (includes the **$X,XXX/year lost revenue** number)

**Step 4:** Screenshot this report or copy the key points—you'll use them in your sales pitch.

### Pro Tips for Audits

**Audit in Batches:** Select 5 leads, run audits, review results, then repeat. Don't audit all 50 leads at once.

**Focus on Revenue Loss:** The **"$X,XXX/year lost revenue"** number is your closing tool. Memorize it for each lead.

**Check Competitor Gaps:** If the audit shows a competitor with 91/100 vs their 68/100, mention this in your pitch ("Your competitor down the street has a modern site and is stealing your customers").

**Save Screenshots:** Take screenshots of the worst parts of their website (broken images, tiny text, confusing navigation). Use these in your email.

## Part 4: Sending Outreach Emails (The First Contact)

### The Email Strategy

Your goal is **not** to sell on the first email. Your goal is to get them on a phone call where you can close the deal. The email should:
1. Show you've done research (mention their business by name)
2. Prove there's a problem (share the prestige score)
3. Quantify the cost (mention the revenue loss)
4. Offer a free consultation (low-pressure next step)

### Step-by-Step: Generating Email Drafts

**Step 1:** Open the lead detail page for an audited lead.

**Step 2:** Click **"Copy Email to Clipboard"** button (green button).

**Step 3:** The system generates a personalized email with:
- Their business name
- Prestige score
- Revenue loss calculation
- Specific issues found (e.g., "missing emergency service CTA")

**Step 4:** Paste the email into Gmail, Outlook, or your email client.

**Step 5:** **Customize it** (see template below).

### Email Template (Customize This!)

```
Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]'s website

Hi [Owner's First Name],

I was researching local [electricians/roofers/HVAC companies] in [City] and came across [Business Name]. I noticed a few things on your website that might be costing you customers.

I ran a quick analysis and found:
- Your site scores 68/100 for digital presence (competitors average 85+)
- Load time is 3.2 seconds (Google recommends under 2 seconds)
- Your phone number isn't clickable on mobile (60% of visitors are on phones)

Based on these issues, you're likely losing around $19,200/year in potential revenue.

I specialize in helping local service businesses like yours recover that lost revenue with modern, mobile-friendly websites. I'd love to show you a quick mockup of what your site could look like—no charge, no obligation.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Phone Number]
```

### Pro Tips for Outreach

**Personalize the Subject Line:** Use their business name. "Quick question about Legacy Heating's website" gets opened more than "Website redesign services".

**Keep It Short:** 5-7 sentences max. Busy business owners don't read long emails.

**Lead with the Problem:** Don't start with "Hi, I'm a web designer." Start with "I noticed your website has X issue."

**Use Their Language:** If they're an HVAC company, say "emergency service calls" not "conversion rate optimization."

**Follow Up Twice:** If no response in 3 days, send a short follow-up: "Hi [Name], just checking if you saw my email about your website. Happy to send over a quick mockup if you're interested."

## Part 5: The Sales Call (Closing the Deal)

### Before the Call

**Prepare Your Pitch:**
1. Open the lead detail page
2. Open the Report Drawer
3. Have the revenue loss number ready ($X,XXX/year)
4. Screenshot 2-3 specific issues on their site

**Set Expectations:**
- This is a **consultation**, not a sales pitch
- You're showing them what's wrong and how to fix it
- The goal is to get them to say "yes" to a proposal

### The 15-Minute Call Script

**Minute 1-2: Build Rapport**
> "Thanks for hopping on! I know you're busy, so I'll keep this quick. How's business been lately?"

*Let them talk. Listen for pain points ("We're not getting enough calls" or "Our competitor is beating us").*

**Minute 3-5: Show the Problem**
> "So I took a look at your website and ran it through our analysis tool. Your site scores 68 out of 100 for digital presence. The main issues are [mention 2-3 specific leaks from the audit report]."

*Share your screen if possible. Show the Report Drawer with the Digital Health Grade.*

**Minute 6-8: Quantify the Cost**
> "Based on your industry and location, these issues are costing you around $19,200 per year in lost revenue. That's about $1,600 per month in customers who visit your site but call your competitor instead."

*Pause. Let this sink in.*

**Minute 9-11: Show the Solution**
> "The good news is, this is fixable. I can rebuild your site with a modern design, make it mobile-friendly, add clear call-to-action buttons, and optimize it for Google. Most of my clients see a 30-40% increase in calls within the first month."

**Minute 12-14: Present the Offer**
> "My standard package is $5,000. That includes:
> - Complete website redesign (modern, mobile-friendly)
> - All content rewritten for clarity
> - Contact forms that go straight to your email
> - Google Maps integration
> - 30 days of free tweaks after launch
>
> I can have it done in 7-10 days. Does that sound like something you'd want to move forward with?"

**Minute 15: Close or Follow Up**

**If they say YES:**
> "Great! I'll send over a simple agreement and invoice today. Once I get the deposit ($2,500 upfront, $2,500 on delivery), I'll start building right away."

**If they say MAYBE:**
> "No problem. How about I send you a quick mockup of what your new site could look like? If you like it, we move forward. If not, no hard feelings."

**If they say NO:**
> "I totally understand. If you change your mind, just shoot me an email. In the meantime, I'd recommend at least making your phone number clickable on mobile—that's an easy fix that'll help right away."

### Handling Objections

**"That's too expensive."**
> "I get it. But think about it this way: if you're losing $1,600/month, this pays for itself in 3 months. After that, it's pure profit."

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Of course. What specifically are you unsure about? The price, the timeline, or something else?"

**"Can you do it for $3,000?"**
> "I can work with you. How about $4,000 if you pay the full amount upfront? That saves me admin time, so I can pass the savings to you."

**"I already have a web guy."**
> "That's great! When was the last time he updated your site? If it's been over 2 years, it might be time for a refresh. Google's ranking algorithm changes constantly."

### Pro Tips for Sales Calls

**Record the Call:** Use Zoom or a call recorder (with permission). Review it later to improve your pitch.

**Don't Oversell:** If they're not interested, let them go. Chasing leads wastes time.

**Offer Payment Plans:** If $5k is too much, offer $2k upfront + $1k/month for 3 months.

**Close Same-Day:** If they say yes, send the invoice immediately. Don't wait—they might change their mind.

## Part 6: Generating the Website (The Delivery)

### What the Website Generator Does

The AI reads the audit report and creates a complete, production-ready website with:
- **Modern design** (clean, professional layout)
- **Mobile-responsive** (works on phones/tablets)
- **Industry-specific colors** (blue for plumbers, green for landscapers, etc.)
- **Clear CTAs** ("Call Now", "Get a Free Quote")
- **Contact forms** (emails go to the client)
- **Google Maps integration** (shows their location)

You don't write any code. The AI does everything.

### Step-by-Step: Generating the Website

**Step 1:** Open the lead detail page for the client you just closed.

**Step 2:** Scroll down to the **"Actions"** section.

**Step 3:** Click **"Generate Website"** button (purple gradient button).

**Step 4:** Wait 60-90 seconds. The AI is:
- Reading the audit report
- Analyzing their current website
- Generating HTML/CSS/JavaScript
- Creating a custom design

**Step 5:** The **Website Editor Modal** will open automatically.

### Step-by-Step: Customizing the Website

The editor has two panels:
- **Left Panel:** Customization controls (colors, content)
- **Right Panel:** Live preview (see changes in real-time)

**Customizing Colors:**

1. **Primary Color:** This is the main brand color (buttons, headers). Click the color picker and choose a color that matches their industry:
   - **Blue (#3b82f6):** Plumbers, electricians, HVAC
   - **Green (#10b981):** Landscapers, lawn care
   - **Red (#ef4444):** Roofers, emergency services
   - **Orange (#f97316):** Auto repair, construction

2. **Secondary Color:** This is the accent color (links, highlights). Choose a complementary color.

3. **Background Color:** Usually white (#ffffff) or light gray (#f3f4f6).

**Customizing Content:**

1. **Business Name:** If the AI got it wrong, type the correct name here.

2. **Headline:** This is the main message on the homepage. Make it clear and benefit-focused:
   - ❌ "Welcome to ABC Plumbing"
   - ✅ "24/7 Emergency Plumbing in [City] – Call Now!"

3. **Services:** List their main services, comma-separated:
   - Example: "Emergency Repairs, Water Heater Installation, Drain Cleaning, Leak Detection"

4. **Contact Info:** Enter their phone number, email, and address.

**Step 6:** As you edit, watch the **Live Preview** on the right. Changes appear instantly.

**Step 7:** When you're happy with the design, click **"Save & Regenerate"** button (bottom left).

**Step 8:** Wait 10-15 seconds for the AI to apply your changes.

### Pro Tips for Customization

**Match Their Brand:** If they have a logo, use a color picker tool (like [Coolors.co](https://coolors.co)) to extract the exact colors from their logo.

**Keep It Simple:** Don't use 5 different colors. Stick to 2-3 colors max.

**Test on Mobile:** After generating, open the preview on your phone to make sure it looks good.

**Ask for Feedback:** Send the client a screenshot before finalizing. Say: "Here's a preview of your new site. Any changes you'd like?"

## Part 7: Downloading and Delivering (The Handoff)

### Step-by-Step: Downloading the Website

**Step 1:** After customizing the website, click **"Download ZIP"** button (green button).

**Step 2:** A ZIP file will download to your computer. It contains:
- `index.html` (the main page)
- `styles.css` (the design)
- `script.js` (any interactive features)

**Step 3:** Unzip the file and open `index.html` in a web browser to preview it one last time.

### Step-by-Step: Delivering to the Client

You have **two options** for delivery:

#### Option A: Host It for Them (Recommended)

**Why This Works:** Most small business owners don't know how to upload files to a web host. If you do it for them, you look like a hero.

**How to Do It:**

1. **Buy a domain** (e.g., `legacyheating.com`) on [Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com) or [Google Domains](https://domains.google.com). Cost: $10-15/year.

2. **Sign up for hosting** on [Netlify](https://www.netlify.com) (free) or [Vercel](https://www.vercel.com) (free).

3. **Upload the website:**
   - Drag the unzipped folder into Netlify's dashboard
   - Netlify will give you a live URL (e.g., `legacyheating.netlify.app`)

4. **Connect the domain:**
   - In Netlify, go to "Domain Settings" and add the custom domain
   - Update the DNS settings in Namecheap to point to Netlify

5. **Send the client the live link** and login credentials.

**Pro Tip:** Charge an extra $500 for hosting setup. Most clients will happily pay this.

#### Option B: Send Them the ZIP File

**Why This Works:** If the client already has a web developer or hosting account, they can handle the upload themselves.

**How to Do It:**

1. Email the ZIP file with these instructions:

```
Hi [Client Name],

Attached is your new website! Here's how to get it live:

1. Unzip the file on your computer
2. Log into your web hosting account (GoDaddy, Bluehost, etc.)
3. Go to the "File Manager" or "FTP" section
4. Upload all the files to the "public_html" folder
5. Your site will be live at your domain within 5 minutes

If you need help with this, I can set it up for you for an additional $500. Just let me know!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Step-by-Step: Collecting Final Payment

**Step 1:** Once the website is live, send the client an email:

```
Hi [Client Name],

Great news—your new website is live at [URL]!

I've also attached a quick guide on how to update your contact info and add new content. If you need any tweaks in the next 30 days, just let me know (that's included in your package).

The final payment of $2,500 is due now. You can pay via:
- Venmo: @YourUsername
- Zelle: your-email@example.com
- Check: [Your Mailing Address]

Thanks for trusting me with your website. Looking forward to seeing your business grow!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

**Step 2:** Wait 2-3 days. If no payment, send a follow-up:

```
Hi [Client Name],

Just checking in—did you get a chance to review the final invoice? Let me know if you have any questions!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Step 3:** If still no payment after 7 days, call them. Most clients just forgot.

### Pro Tips for Delivery

**Offer a "Maintenance Plan":** After delivery, offer $99/month for ongoing updates (change photos, update text, add new pages). This creates recurring revenue.

**Ask for a Testimonial:** Once they're happy, ask: "Would you mind writing a quick review on Google? It really helps my business." Use these testimonials in future pitches.

**Request Referrals:** Say: "If you know any other [electricians/roofers/plumbers] who need a website, I'd love an introduction. I'll give you $500 for every referral that closes."

## Part 9: Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue: "The audit failed"

**Cause:** The lead's website might be offline, or the screenshot capture failed.

**Fix:**
1. Go to the Leads page
2. Click "Capture Screenshot" button on the lead
3. Wait 20 seconds
4. Try auditing again

---

### Issue: "The website generator is taking too long"

**Cause:** The AI is processing a complex website with lots of content.

**Fix:**
- Wait up to 2 minutes
- If it still doesn't work, refresh the page and try again
- If it fails twice, contact support

---

### Issue: "The client doesn't like the design"

**Cause:** Everyone has different taste.

**Fix:**
1. Ask: "What specifically don't you like? The colors, the layout, or the content?"
2. Open the Website Editor Modal
3. Change the colors to match their preference
4. Click "Save & Regenerate"
5. Send them a new screenshot

**Pro Tip:** Offer 2 free revisions. After that, charge $200 per additional revision.

---

### Issue: "The client says their competitor's site is better"

**Cause:** They're comparing your work to a $20k custom site.

**Fix:**
- Show them the Report Drawer and point out that their competitor's site scores 91/100 vs their old site's 68/100
- Say: "Your new site will score 85-90/100, which puts you in the same league as your competitor—at a fraction of the cost."

---

### Issue: "I can't find good leads in my area"

**Cause:** You might be in a small town, or the category is saturated.

**Fix:**
- Expand your search radius (try nearby cities)
- Switch categories (if electricians are saturated, try roofers)
- Target businesses with 10-50 employees (they have money but no in-house web team)

**Short Answer:** Not immediately, but yes eventually.

**Why:**
- Protects your personal assets if a client sues you
- Makes you look more professional
- Easier to open a business bank account

**How:**
- Use [LegalZoom](https://www.legalzoom.com) or [Incfile](https://www.incfile.com)
- Cost: $100-300
- Takes 1-2 weeks

Services:

### When You Feel Stuck
